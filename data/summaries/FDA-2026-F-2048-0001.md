# Public Summary: FDA-2026-F-2048-0001

## What This Document Does
- Announces FDA has filed a food additive petition (FAP 2325) from Kemin Industries to amend 21 CFR part 573 to allow chromium propionate as a chromium source in feed for layer and breeding chickens.
- Makes the petitioner's environmental assessment (EA) available for public review and comment in docket FDA-2026-F-2048.
- Signals procedural next steps: FDA will review the EA under NEPA and, if it finds no Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) is required and the petition is approved, may issue a Finding of No Significant Impact (FONSI) when/if it publishes a final regulatory action.

## Why It Matters
- Regulatory change: If approved, chromium propionate would become an approved chromium source in layer and breeder feeds, affecting feed formulation and sourcing decisions.
- Environmental and public-health implications: Use of a new chromium compound in feed can alter chromium inputs to poultry manure, soils, and potentially affect water, wildlife, and human exposure pathways—issues typically evaluated in the EA.
- Precedent and oversight: This stage is a key opportunity to test whether FDA’s environmental review is transparent, methodologically sound, and considers realistic exposure and cumulative-impact scenarios before any approval decision is made.

## Who Is Affected
- Poultry producers (layer and breeder operations) and feed manufacturers who may adopt the additive.
- Kemin Industries and competing additive suppliers.
- Environmental and public-health groups monitoring trace-metal inputs from agriculture.
- Communities near feed mills, poultry farms, and land-application areas for manure.
- State regulators and extension services that advise on manure management and soil health.
- Researchers and labs involved in residue testing, environmental monitoring, and poultry nutrition.

## Issues Worth Commenting On (0-6)
1. EA analytical boundaries and scope
   - Why it matters: A narrow geographic, temporal, or media scope can undercount potential impacts (soil, water, air, long-term accumulation).
   - Practical question to ask: Does the EA clearly define its geographic coverage, time horizon, affected environmental media (soil, surface and groundwater, air), and waste/manure pathways? If not, what expanded boundaries should FDA require?

2. Exposure pathways and cumulative impacts
   - Why it matters: Chromium from this additive would add to existing chromium sources; cumulative effects could be materially different from single-source assessments.
   - Practical question to ask: Does the EA evaluate cumulative chromium exposures from current feed additives, fertilizer, industrial sources, and natural background? If not, request specific cumulative-impact scenarios and data sources.

3. Alternatives and mitigation measures
   - Why it matters: NEPA requires consideration of reasonable alternatives and mitigation; absence of clear alternatives or mitigation weakens environmental review.
   - Practical question to ask: What alternatives to approving chromium propionate were analyzed (e.g., use restrictions, application limits, monitoring requirements), and what mitigation measures does the EA propose if approvals proceed?

4. Treatment of uncertainty and data quality
   - Why it matters: Environmental analyses depend on assumptions about uptake, persistence, and bioavailability; poor treatment of uncertainty can mask material risks.
   - Practical question to ask: How does the EA quantify uncertainty and sensitivity to key assumptions (e.g., bioavailability in manure/amended soils)? Request transparency on data gaps and conservative assumptions.

5. Post-approval monitoring and reporting conditions
   - Why it matters: If approval relies on modeled assumptions, monitoring is often necessary to detect unexpected environmental effects and to validate risk predictions.
   - Practical question to ask: Will FDA require post-approval monitoring (e.g., soil and water testing near operations using chromium propionate) and reporting obligations for the petitioner or users? If so, what metrics and timelines?
