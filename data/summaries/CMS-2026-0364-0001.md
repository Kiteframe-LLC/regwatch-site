# Public Summary: CMS-2026-0364-0001

_Source file: `summaries/CMS-2026-0364-0001.md`_
_Detected type: `NPRM`_

## What This Document Does
- Proposes revisions to the Conditions for Coverage for Organ Procurement Organizations (OPOs).
- Adds new Quality Assessment Performance Improvement (QAPI) requirements focused on medically complex organs and donors.
- Revises how OPO designation and service-area competition are determined and clarifies when an area is open for competition.
- Updates the appeals process for designation decisions while retaining the existing tiered performance structure.

## Why It Matters
- Changes shift the compliance landscape for OPOs by adding procedural requirements that may increase administrative work.
- The rule aims to raise organ procurement and transplant performance, but the provided record does not clearly show empirical links from the proposed procedures to better outcomes.
- Clarifications on competition and successor selection could cause operational disruption if transitions are not carefully managed.
- Gaps in analysis (burden estimates, causal evidence, alternatives) create meaningful points for public comment and potential legal challenge.

## Who Is Affected
- Organ Procurement Organizations (primary regulated entities) — new QAPI obligations and potential redesignation risk.
- Transplant hospitals and clinicians — changes in OPO behavior and regional supply may affect organ availability.
- Patients awaiting transplants and donor families — potential downstream effects on organ recovery and allocation.
- CMS and contractors — will implement and monitor the revised Conditions for Coverage.

## Issues Worth Commenting On (0-6)
1. Issue: Lack of demonstrated causal link between QAPI expansion and improved transplant outcomes
   - Why it matters: Mandating additional QAPI processes could increase administrative burden without measurable benefit to recovery or transplant rates.
   - Practical question to ask: Please provide empirical evidence or modeling showing that the specific QAPI elements proposed lead to improved donor conversion rates, organ utilization, or transplant outcomes.

2. Issue: No visible analysis of transition risks when an OPO service area is opened for competition or a successor is selected
   - Why it matters: Replacing or competing OPOs can disrupt organ recovery operations, potentially reducing available organs during the transition period.
   - Practical question to ask: Please provide risk modeling or case studies addressing how CMS will minimize disruption to organ recovery during OPO transitions and the expected impact on organ procurement volumes.

3. Issue: Alternatives and less-burdensome options are not evaluated
   - Why it matters: APA requires consideration of reasonable alternatives; absence of such analysis raises procedural and legal concerns and may mean more intrusive approaches were adopted without justification.
   - Practical question to ask: Why were phased implementation, targeted interventions for low-performing OPOs, or incentive-based approaches not considered or documented in the record?

4. Issue: No administrative burden or cost estimates shown for new requirements
   - Why it matters: OPOs may need staff, training, or systems to meet QAPI expansion; lack of burden estimates prevents informed assessment of feasibility and impact.
   - Practical question to ask: Please publish estimated administrative and operational costs for compliance (staff hours, IT changes, training) and the basis for those estimates.

5. Issue: Potential interaction between new processes and the existing tiered performance metrics is unspecified
   - Why it matters: New procedural requirements may change reporting, behavior, or metrics in ways that make current tiers less valid or create perverse incentives (metric optimization over outcomes).
   - Practical question to ask: Provide analysis showing how proposed QAPI and designation changes interact with the tiered metric system and whether metrics remain reliable and valid after implementation.
