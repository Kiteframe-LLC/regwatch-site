# Public Summary: NHTSA-2026-0629-0001

## What This Document Does
- Proposes to except automated driving system (ADS) vehicles without manual controls from certain driver-visibility Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS No. 103 & No. 104).
- Proposes updating test procedures by incorporating SAE J902 (2025).
- Frames the change as removing driver-centric requirements because ADS vehicles do not rely on a human driver for forward visibility.
- Asserts expected cost reductions and no substantive adverse safety effects, while relying on manufacturers’ obligations and general vehicle-defect authority to address safety.

## Why It Matters
- Safety scope: The rule reframes visibility requirements as solely driver-centric, which may overlook other safety roles of windshield and wipers (occupant egress, emergency responder actions, sensor performance, and redundancy).
- Evidence gap: The docket lacks supporting technical, human‑factors, or cost analyses, increasing the risk that key assumptions are unsupported.
- Incentives and design: Removing prescriptive requirements could encourage manufacturers to eliminate windshield systems or underinvest in redundancy, shifting oversight from ex ante standards to ex post defect enforcement.
- Legal vulnerability: Thin analytical support and missing alternatives increase the chance of successful procedural or substantive challenges to the rule.

## Who Is Affected
- Primary: Manufacturers of ADS-equipped vehicles that lack manual driving controls.
- Secondary: Suppliers of windshields, wiper/defogging systems, sensor and camera vendors.
- Public stakeholders: Fleet operators, first responders, riders/occupants, and road-users in mixed fleets.
- Regulators and safety advocates monitoring ADS deployment and mixed-traffic transitions.

## Issues Worth Commenting On (0-6)
1. Issue: The safety justification is thin and largely assertion-based.
   - Why it matters: The core claim that the exception will have “no substantive adverse safety effects” drives the rule; if unsupported, the agency’s safety determination may be legally vulnerable.
   - Practical comment question: What empirical evidence (crash data, human-factors studies, or failure-mode analyses) supports NHTSA’s tentative finding that excepting ADS vehicles will not materially affect safety?

2. Issue: Lack of analysis for mixed fleets and transition states.
   - Why it matters: ADS vehicles will share roads with manually driven vehicles for the foreseeable future; interactions and edge cases (manual takeover, emergency egress) may rely on visibility systems.
   - Practical comment question: Has NHTSA assessed safety risks and mitigation needs in mixed-operation environments where ADS and manual-control vehicles coexist?

3. Issue: No meaningful evaluation of alternatives (partial or conditional approaches).
   - Why it matters: A binary “exempt vs. not exempt” approach may overlook feasible middle options (performance-based visibility requirements, sensor-equivalence tests, conditional exemptions tied to operational design domains).
   - Practical comment question: What analyses did NHTSA perform comparing full exemption to partial or conditional standards (e.g., sensor‑performance criteria, operational limits)?

4. Issue: Unsupported economic assertions about cost reductions.
   - Why it matters: The agency’s Regulatory Flexibility Act and cost-benefit obligations rely on substantiated economic analysis; unquantified cost claims weaken that foundation.
   - Practical comment question: Please provide the cost estimates, assumptions, and methodology supporting any claims that the proposal will reduce regulatory costs.

5. Issue: Reliance on post‑market defect enforcement instead of prescriptive design requirements.
   - Why it matters: Ex post enforcement can be slower and less preventive than design standards; this may reduce incentives to design redundancy into critical sensor/visibility systems.
   - Practical comment question: How does NHTSA evaluate whether defect enforcement will achieve the same level of systemic safety as prescriptive FMVSS requirements?
