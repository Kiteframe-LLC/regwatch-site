## What This Document Does
- Proposes amending 12 C.F.R. § 741.5 to remove the current federal 30‑day minimum notice requirement for terminating excess share insurance.
- Replaces the fixed “at least 30 days before” standard with a flexible requirement that Federally Insured Credit Unions (FICUs) notify members “prior to the effective date of termination.”
- Explains the change as increasing flexibility, aligning timing with state law and contractual arrangements, and leaving timing decisions to each FICU’s board.
- States the Board does not expect material changes to FICUs’ obligations, asserts consideration of small‑entity impacts (RFA), and says the rule does not create PRA burdens. The supplied docket materials contain the proposed rule text and preamble only; no supporting studies or examples were provided in the record supplied here.

## Why It Matters
- Member protection: The timing of notice affects members’ ability to respond to loss of excess insurance (e.g., move uninsured funds).
- Regulatory floor vs. local discretion: Removing a federal minimum shifts responsibility to local boards and contracts, potentially creating uneven practices.
- Small entities: NCUA certifies no material impact on small credit unions, but the underlying facts supporting that certification are not shown in the provided materials.
- Legal/administrative risk: A thin explanatory record may invite procedural challenges under the Administrative Procedure Act if the agency does not adequately justify replacing a bright‑line rule with a flexible standard.
- Practical incentives: Without a minimum floor, some institutions might delay notice until the last practicable moment while still being "prior," which could disadvantage members.

## Who Is Affected
- Federally insured credit unions (FICUs) that carry excess share insurance above NCUSIF limits.
- Credit union members with deposits exceeding NCUSIF coverage who rely on excess insurance.
- Potentially state regulators and parties to relevant member agreements, to the extent timing interacts with state law or contract terms.

## Issues Worth Commenting On (0-6)
1. Issue: Adequacy of the record supporting removal of the 30‑day floor
   - Why it matters: The proposal’s main justification rests on asserted flexibility and alignment benefits, but the supplied materials lack examples, data, or state‑law comparisons showing a real problem.
   - Practical question to ask: Please provide concrete examples, complaint or supervisory history, and any state‑law or contractual conflicts that demonstrate why the 30‑day floor is unworkable.

2. Issue: Practical sufficiency of “prior notice” for member protection
   - Why it matters: Legal notice that arrives immediately before termination may be technically compliant yet leave members no meaningful time to act.
   - Practical question to ask: What standard of timing does NCUA consider “adequate” in practice, and how will the agency ensure members have reasonable time to respond in non‑routine cases?

3. Issue: Consideration of intermediate alternatives
   - Why it matters: The agency does not show analysis of less drastic options (e.g., shorter fixed floor, exception process, or enhanced notice content) that might balance flexibility and member protection.
   - Practical question to ask: Did NCUA evaluate alternatives such as a 10–15 day minimum, an exception pathway, or enhanced disclosure requirements when shorter notice is used?

4. Issue: Support for the Regulatory Flexibility Act (RFA) certification
