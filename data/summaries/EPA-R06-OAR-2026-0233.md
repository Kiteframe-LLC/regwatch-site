## What This Document Does
- Proposes to amend 40 CFR part 62 (subpart T) to reflect EPA’s receipt and acceptance of a CAA section 111(d)/129 “negative declaration” from Louisiana.
- States that Louisiana submitted a letter (dated November 3, 2025) certifying that no existing municipal or institutional solid waste incineration (OSWI) units subject to the OSWI Emission Guidelines exist within the “specified jurisdictions” in the State.
- EPA’s action is administrative: if accepted, EPA will record that Louisiana has no covered existing OSWI units and therefore no state plan under sections 111(d)/129 is required for those jurisdictions.

## Why It Matters
- The NPRM affects whether Louisiana must submit a state plan (or EPA must implement a federal plan) under CAA sections 111(d) and 129 for existing OSWI sources.
- If the negative declaration is correct, no additional regulatory requirements will be imposed for existing OSWI units in the covered jurisdictions.
- The central public-interest question is evidentiary: does the public record adequately show that Louisiana searched for and correctly identified all sources that could now be covered after the 2025 OSWI applicability changes?
- A thin factual record can let a newly-covered, borderline, inactive, or misclassified facility escape the 111(d)/129 planning framework unintentionally.

## Who Is Affected
- Operators or owners of municipal, institutional, or other solid-waste incineration units in Louisiana that could be subject to the OSWI Emission Guidelines after the 2025 revisions.
- Local governments or institutions (e.g., municipalities, military bases, universities) that operate small-scale incinerators.
- Environmental and public-interest groups monitoring compliance with CAA section 111(d)/129.
- EPA and LDEQ (state regulator) insofar as the rule documents the state’s determination and EPA’s acceptance.

## Issues Worth Commenting On (0-6)
1. Issue: Lack of documented screening methodology
   - Why it matters: The record says LDEQ “reviewed its sources” but does not explain how it searched, what databases or permit records were used, or what criteria screened facilities in or out. Without method details, the public cannot verify the negative-declaration conclusion.
   - Practical question to ask: Please provide a short description of the databases, permit files, emissions inventories, or other records LDEQ consulted and the criteria used to identify potential OSWI units.

2. Issue: Whether the review accounted for the 2025 revisions to OSWI applicability
   - Why it matters: Applicability changes in 2025 may expand or alter which units are covered. If LDEQ used pre-2025 assumptions, some facilities could be misclassified as out of scope.
   - Practical question to ask: Did LDEQ explicitly evaluate facilities against the 2025 OSWI Emission Guideline applicability language; if so, please describe how newly-implicated source categories were considered.

3. Issue: Geographic and jurisdictional clarity of the “specified jurisdictions” language
   - Why it matters: The negative declaration refers to “specified jurisdictions in Louisiana,” but the NPRM does not detail which parishes/areas were included or excluded. That affects which facilities remain subject to CAA planning obligations.
   - Practical question to ask: Please identify the specific geographic jurisdictions covered by the negative declaration and clarify whether any areas or facility types were excluded.

4. Issue: Transparency and record completeness
   - Why it matters: A short supplemental memo would let the public assess the reliability of the state’s finding and reduce the risk that covered units remain unregulated due to a cursory review.
   - Practical question to ask: Will EPA request or place in the docket a brief LDEQ memorandum summarizing the source inventory and screening steps used to reach the negative declaration?

If no major issues are identified from the provided record: No — the record identifies the gaps above.
