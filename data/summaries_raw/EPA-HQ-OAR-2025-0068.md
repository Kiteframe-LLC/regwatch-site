## Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

### Claim: Title V removal does not affect compliance

* **Underlying assumption**

  * Existing opacity monitoring + enforcement is sufficient

* **Failure scenario**

  * Increased number of small/mobile ACIs → reduced oversight visibility

* **Procedural implication**

  * Monitoring sufficiency becomes **central record vulnerability**

---

### Claim: Emissions unchanged

* **Underlying assumption**

  * Behavioral response (increased ACI use) is negligible

* **Failure scenario**

  * Disaster-use expansion + permitting removal → increased utilization

* **Procedural implication**

  * Requires **activity-level modeling or bounding analysis**

---

### Claim: Temporary-use exemption justified

* **Underlying assumption**

  * disaster debris burning < alternative harms (wildfire, decomposition)

* **Failure scenario**

  * prolonged “temporary” use → de facto regulatory bypass

* **Procedural implication**

  * duration + scope constraints become critical

---

## Step 2 — Essence of the Rule

### Observed Facts

* Consolidates ACI opacity rules into single subpart
* Removes Title V permitting for non-major ACI
* Allows temporary CISWI use during disasters without §129 compliance
* Retains opacity limits (10% / 35%)

### Inference

* This is a **deregulatory simplification + emergency-use expansion rule**
