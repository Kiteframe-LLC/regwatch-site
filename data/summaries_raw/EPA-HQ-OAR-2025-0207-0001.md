# Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

## Claim: HAP Applicability Thresholds Reasonable

Underlying assumption
Existing thresholds still capture the relevant emissions profile.

Failure scenario

* emissions distribution has shifted
* smaller terminals emit disproportionately
* technology costs have fallen

Procedural implication

Failure to reconsider threshold could under-regulate sources.

---

## Claim: Vapor Pressure Exemption Should Remain

Underlying assumption

Cost > $100k/ton means regulation is unreasonable.

Failure scenario

* EPA cost benchmark inconsistent with other rules
* local health impacts significant
* cumulative emissions underestimated

Procedural implication

Cost-effectiveness threshold becomes litigation focal point.

---

## Claim: Periodic Testing Improves Control Performance

Underlying assumption

Testing frequency materially improves operational performance.

Failure scenario

* control efficiency already stable
* marginal benefit small
* facilities game test timing

Procedural implication

Testing requirement becomes administrative burden with limited environmental gain.
