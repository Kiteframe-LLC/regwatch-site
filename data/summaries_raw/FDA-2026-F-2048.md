## Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

### Major claim: Phase I exposure is complete because PEC is below 100 ppb
- **Underlying assumption:** The 100 ppb threshold is the correct and sufficient decision gate for this use case.
- **Failure scenario:** Soil/manure fate, speciation, or localized loading differ from assumptions; modeled concentration remains below threshold on average but misses hotspot conditions.
- **Procedural implication:** FDA should explain threshold selection and whether any site-specific or scenario sensitivity is required.

### Major claim: Worst-case assumptions are protective
- **Underlying assumption:** Inputs (feed intake, manure handling, application, fraction treated) and simplifications are conservative in aggregate.
- **Failure scenario:** "Worst-case" is mixed with optimistic simplifications (uniform behavior, linear handling, no atypical operation); net conservatism is not demonstrated.
- **Procedural implication:** Request sensitivity table and uncertainty bounds.

### Major claim: No-action is the only alternative
- **Underlying assumption:** No middle options are reasonable (e.g., conditional approval, monitoring conditions, narrower application constraints).
- **Failure scenario:** Feasible partial alternatives exist and are standard in risk management.
- **Procedural implication:** Alternatives discussion may be underdeveloped for commenter evaluation.

### Major claim: Existing approvals in broilers/turkeys/horses support this extension
- **Underlying assumption:** Cross-species extrapolation and cumulative framing are adequate for layers/breeders at proposed inclusion rate.
- **Failure scenario:** Operational differences (production duration, manure pathways, regional concentration) alter environmental profile.
- **Procedural implication:** FDA should require explicit comparability rationale.
