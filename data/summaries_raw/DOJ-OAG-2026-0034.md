# Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

### Claim

Chapter 154 has never been applied because prior certification standards imposed unauthorized requirements.

**Underlying assumption**

Judicial and prior DOJ interpretations imposed extrastatutory barriers.

**Failure scenario**

If the real cause is state noncompliance with statutory counsel standards rather than regulatory barriers, removing regulatory requirements will not produce certifications.

**Procedural implication**

Rule may fail to achieve stated objective (expedited habeas review).

---

### Claim

Reducing certification barriers will meaningfully reduce capital case delays.

**Underlying assumption**

Federal habeas timelines are primarily driven by certification eligibility.

**Failure scenario**

Most delay occurs in litigation unrelated to Chapter 154 eligibility.

**Procedural implication**

Rule’s justification may lack causal evidence.

---

**Underlying assumption**

# Step 2 — Essence of the Rule

## Observed Facts

* DOJ proposes revisions to regulations implementing **28 U.S.C. Chapter 154**. 
* Chapter 154 provides **expedited federal habeas review** for death-sentenced prisoners when states meet counsel-system standards. 
* DOJ proposes to **remove regulatory requirements not explicitly found in statute**. 
* Changes affect:

  * counsel competency standards
  * counsel compensation
  * appointment timing
  * definition of indigent prisoners
  * DOJ internal review process
  * certification finality

## Inference

The rule attempts to **increase the number of states eligible for Chapter 154 certification**, thereby accelerating federal habeas review in capital cases.

# Step 18 — Decision Brief

### Leverage Point

Sentence:

> “Nearly 30 years after the original enactment of chapter 154, its expedited federal habeas corpus procedures have not been applied…” 

### Routine Description

The rule argues that the absence of Chapter 154 certifications demonstrates regulatory over-restriction. However, the NPRM does not provide analysis of whether state capital counsel systems actually satisfy statutory standards or whether litigation barriers prevented certification.

### Regulatory / Legal Context

Under APA review, agencies must demonstrate that regulatory changes are supported by reasoned analysis and evidence connecting the rule to the stated problem.

### Real-World Consequence

If DOJ removes regulatory safeguards without demonstrating that they caused the lack of certification, courts could invalidate the rule as insufficiently justified.

### Worth a Comment?

**High value**
