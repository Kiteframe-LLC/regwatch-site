## Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

### Claim 1

EPA asserts the plan **provides for maintenance of the 2006 PM₂.₅ standard**.

**Underlying assumption**

Ambient PM₂.₅ levels will remain below the NAAQS without additional regulatory measures.

**Failure scenario**

Economic growth, transportation shifts, or wildfire smoke could elevate PM₂.₅ concentrations above modeled expectations.

**Procedural implication**

Contingency provisions must be robust enough to trigger corrective measures.

---

### Claim 2

The area **qualifies for the Limited Maintenance Plan option**.

**Underlying assumption**

Observed concentrations remain sufficiently below the NAAQS threshold to justify simplified planning requirements.

**Failure scenario**

Monitoring variability could push design values near the standard, undermining the “limited” approach.

**Procedural implication**

EPA should demonstrate the margin below the standard and its statistical robustness.

---

### Claim 3

Transportation conformity is adequately addressed.

**Underlying assumption**

Future transportation emissions will not materially increase PM₂.₅ concentrations.

**Failure scenario**

Large infrastructure expansions could increase emissions despite conformity assumptions.

**Procedural implication**

Transportation conformity adequacy should include updated emissions forecasts.
