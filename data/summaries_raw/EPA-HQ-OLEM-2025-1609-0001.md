# TL;DR (Actionable Read)

**This is a structurally normal EPA NPRM with a real analytical spine, but it contains a few high-leverage comment points:**

1. **Core move:** EPA reclassifies *recovered abandoned tires* as non-waste fuel in cement kilns.
2. **Primary gap:** The rule hinges on **equivalence to collected tires** but provides limited *empirical demonstration of equivalence after recovery*.
3. **Best comment angle:** Ask for **boundary conditions and verification standards** for “managed as a valuable commodity” and “recovery processing.”
4. **Score:** **4.5 / 10 procedural concern** → not weak, but with meaningful clarification opportunities.

## Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

### Claim: “Recovered tires ≈ collected tires”

* **Underlying assumption:** Recovery removes meaningful contamination variability
* **Failure scenario:**

  * Tires in long-term piles may have:

    * soil/oil contamination
    * chemical degradation
    * commingled waste residues
* **Procedural implication:**
  If variability exists, categorical determination may overgeneralize.

---

### Claim: “Recovery = sufficient processing”

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Removal + sorting achieves equivalence to non-discarded material

* **Adversarial interpretation:**
  Minimal “recovery” could be claimed to bypass shredding/dewiring

* **Procedural implication:**
  Ambiguity in processing definition → enforcement variability

---

### Claim: “Processing requirement is unnecessary”

* **Counterfactual:**
  If processing *does* reduce emissions or variability, removal of requirement could increase risk

* **Implication:**
  Requires stronger comparative evidence than provided in-text

## Step 18 — Decision Brief

### Leverage Point: “Recovered = equivalent to collected”

* **Key sentence:**
  “Recovered scrap tires are largely physically and chemically identical…” 

* **Routine context:**
  This claim underpins the categorical reclassification under RCRA/CAA boundary.

* **Regulatory significance:**
  Determines whether combustion is regulated as:

  * waste incineration (strict)
  * fuel use (less strict)

* **Real-world consequence:**
  Overbroad equivalence → potential admission of contaminated materials without processing safeguards

* **Worth a comment:** **High**

* **Comment posture:** **evidence request + boundary clarification**
