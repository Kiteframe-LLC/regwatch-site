## Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

### Claim: Underutilized quota justifies loosening restrictions

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Low harvest = overly restrictive regulation (not demand, cost, or stock distribution)

* **Failure scenario:**
  Low harvest due to:

  * market demand
  * fleet capacity
  * fuel cost
  * regulatory complexity unrelated to boundary

* **Procedural implication:**
  Rule may **misdiagnose problem**, weakening rational connection

---

### Claim: Boundary removal reduces dead discards

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Discards are driven primarily by boundary restriction

* **Failure scenario:**
  Discards driven by:

  * bycatch rates
  * gear selectivity
  * enforcement risk
  * permit structure

* **Procedural implication:**
  Unsupported ecological benefit → APA vulnerability

---

### Claim: Flexibility improves quota utilization

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Flexibility → behavioral response by fishermen

* **Failure scenario:**
  Fishermen do not respond (economic or operational constraints)

* **Adversarial interpretation:**
  Industry could:

  * increase catch rapidly late season
  * pressure in-season adjustments

* **Procedural implication:**
  Potential for **quota overshoot or uneven exploitation**
