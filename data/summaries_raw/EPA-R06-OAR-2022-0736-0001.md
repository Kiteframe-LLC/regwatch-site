# Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

---

## Claim: Oklahoma reasonably determined emission reduction measures

Underlying assumption:

* State analysis identified **all relevant sources and feasible controls**.

Failure scenario:

* Major emission sources excluded from control analysis
* Cost assumptions understate feasible reductions
* Visibility modeling assumptions inaccurate

Procedural implication:

* If true, EPA approval may rely on **incomplete source analysis**.

---

## Claim: Reasonable progress achieved

Underlying assumption:

* Baseline emissions and visibility trends were correctly modeled.

Failure scenario:

* Emission inventories inaccurate
* Visibility improvement attributed to unrelated external reductions

Procedural implication:

* Reasonable progress determination could be **unsupported by data**.

---

## Claim: “Reasonable progress” ≠ maximal progress

Underlying assumption:

* EPA interpretation of the statute prioritizes cost-reasonable improvements rather than maximum feasible reductions.

Failure scenario:

* Courts interpret Clean Air Act visibility goal more strictly.

Procedural implication:

* Legal vulnerability if statutory interpretation is contested.
