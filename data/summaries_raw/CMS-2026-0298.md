## Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

### Claim: Domestic procurement improves safety

* **Underlying assumption:** Domestic supply chains are more reliable in crisis
* **Failure scenario:** Domestic capacity insufficient → shortages worsen
* **Counterfactual:** Mixed sourcing (domestic + allied imports) may outperform
* **Adversarial interpretation:** Hospitals comply formally but stockpile low-quality domestic substitutes
* **Procedural implication:** Requires empirical support and sensitivity analysis

---

### Claim: Mandating domestic sourcing is appropriate

* **Underlying assumption:** Policy cost is justified by resilience gain
* **Failure scenario:** Costs increase → hospitals reduce service capacity
* **Adversarial interpretation:** Suppliers raise prices due to reduced competition
* **Procedural implication:** Requires cost-benefit analysis

---

### Claim: Scope limited to Medicare hospitals is sufficient

* **Underlying assumption:** Medicare participation is an effective regulatory lever
* **Failure scenario:** Supply chains operate outside Medicare boundaries
* **Procedural implication:** Requires system-level analysis
