## Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

---

### Claim: PMA requirement effective date is appropriate

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Devices in this category present sufficient risk to require PMA-level scrutiny

* **Failure scenario:**
  If actual clinical risk is low or heterogeneous, PMA may be overbroad

* **Counterfactual:**
  Subclasses of devices or use contexts may not justify full PMA

* **Procedural implication:**
  Agency must justify uniform classification or consider stratification

---

### Claim: Scope (blood irradiators “intended to prevent metastasis”)

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Intended use is clearly identifiable and enforceable

* **Failure scenario:**
  Manufacturers adjust labeling to avoid PMA classification

* **Adversarial interpretation:**
  “Prevent metastasis” could be interpreted narrowly to evade regulation

* **Procedural implication:**
  FDA may need clearer definitional boundaries or functional criteria

---

### Claim: Effective date selection is reasonable

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Industry can comply within the proposed timeline

* **Failure scenario:**
  PMA preparation timelines exceed FDA assumptions

* **Counterfactual:**
  Smaller manufacturers face disproportionate burden

* **Procedural implication:**
  Need for phased compliance or transitional mechanisms
