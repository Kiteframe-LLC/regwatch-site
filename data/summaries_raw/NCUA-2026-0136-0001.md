## Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

### Claim: Borrowing limit unnecessary for FISCUs

* **Underlying assumption**

  * State regulation + supervision ≈ equivalent risk control

* **Failure scenario**

  * State regimes vary; some allow higher leverage without equivalent oversight

* **Procedural implication**

  * Potential **unacknowledged federal floor removal** without comparative analysis

---

### Claim: Supervision can replace rule

* **Underlying assumption**

  * Examinations detect excessive borrowing before risk materializes

* **Failure scenario**

  * Rapid borrowing expansion between exam cycles

* **Procedural implication**

  * Need for **timeliness and monitoring evidence**

---

### Claim: Few waiver requests → low impact

* **Underlying assumption**

  * Low waiver usage = low demand for higher borrowing

* **Failure scenario**

  * Waiver friction suppressed demand (latent demand unobserved)

* **Procedural implication**

  * Possible **measurement bias**

---

## Step 2 — Essence of the Rule

### Observed Facts

* Removes §741.2 borrowing cap for all FICUs. 
* Statutory 50% limit remains for FCUs. 
* FISCUs would no longer be subject to federal borrowing cap or waiver process. 

### Inference

* This is a **federal deregulatory shift for state-chartered institutions**, moving control to:

  * state law
  * supervisory processes
