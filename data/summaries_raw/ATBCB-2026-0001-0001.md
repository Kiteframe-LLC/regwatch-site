## Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

### Claim: Floor-use risk justifies rule

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Floor-based care is common and materially harmful

* **Failure scenario:**
  If prevalence is low or mitigated by existing facilities, justification weakens

* **Procedural implication:**
  Vulnerable to **“failure to quantify problem”** challenge

---

### Claim: Incorporate ICC standard vs create new

* **Underlying assumption:**
  ICC standard is sufficiently robust and representative

* **Failure scenario:**
  ICC standard reflects limited stakeholder set or outdated designs

* **Adversarial interpretation:**
  Industry could push incorporation to avoid stricter federal standard

* **Procedural implication:**
  Requires **comparative analysis of standards**

---

### Claim: Continuous adjustability

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Continuous adjustability materially improves accessibility outcomes

* **Failure scenario:**
  Cost or engineering constraints outweigh marginal benefit

* **Procedural implication:**
  Needs **cost–benefit or feasibility support**

## Step 18 — Decision Brief

### Leverage Point 1: Problem Quantification

* **Key sentence:**
  “individuals… will often have to lie on the floor…” 

* **Routine description:**
  Rule relies on asserted harm without quantified prevalence or severity.

* **Implications:**

  * **Regulatory effectiveness:** unclear targeting
  * **Procedural adequacy:** weak evidentiary basis
  * **Statutory compliance:** potentially sufficient but thin

* **Real-world consequence:**
  Standard may be over- or under-designed relative to actual need.

* **Worth a comment:** **High**

---

### Leverage Point 2: ICC vs Federal Standard Choice

* **Key sentence:**
  “considering… incorporate by reference section 613.4…” 

* **Routine description:**
  Agency proposes adopting industry standard without comparative analysis.

* **Implications:**

  * Risk of **delegating policy judgment to private standard**
  * Potential under- or over-inclusiveness

* **Real-world consequence:**
  Locks in design constraints nationwide without federal justification.

* **Worth a comment:** **High**

---

### Leverage Point 3: Height Adjustability Requirement

* **Key sentence:**
  “considering requiring… continuously adjustable…” 

* **Routine description:**
  Technical requirement proposed without cost or usability analysis.

* **Implications:**

  * Engineering feasibility unclear
  * Cost burden unknown

* **Real-world consequence:**
  Could drive up costs or reduce adoption.

* **Worth a comment:** **Moderate–High**
