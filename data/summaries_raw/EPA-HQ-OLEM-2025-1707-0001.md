# Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

## Claim A

**Claim**

Implementation challenges exist with the 2024 rule.

### Underlying assumption

Facilities or regulators have experienced difficulty complying with or interpreting the rule.

### Failure scenario

If implementation challenges are minimal or localized:

* justification for reconsideration weakens
* the record may appear **policy-driven rather than problem-driven**

### Procedural implication

EPA may need:

* evidence of compliance difficulty
* stakeholder feedback
* documented implementation barriers

---

## Claim B

**Claim**

Reducing regulatory burden can be done while maintaining protection.

### Underlying assumption

Current rule contains **inefficiencies rather than core protective features**.

### Counterfactual

If the burden stems from:

* inherently necessary safety planning
* statutory requirements

then burden reduction may weaken compliance protections.

### Procedural implication

EPA will need:

* burden quantification
* evidence that reduced requirements preserve response capacity.
