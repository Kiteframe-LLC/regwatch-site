## Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

### Claim: “No adverse safety effects”

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Driver visibility is irrelevant when no human driver exists.

* **Failure scenario:**

  * ADS failure requiring human intervention
  * Emergency responders relying on visibility
  * Occupant situational awareness (egress, hazard perception)

* **Procedural implication:**
  Safety justification may be **under-scoped (driver-only framing)**

---

### Claim: “No explicit safety need”

* **Underlying assumption:**
  FMVSS 103/104 serve only driver vision

* **Failure scenario:**

  * Visibility supports **non-driving safety functions**
  * Sensor redundancy depends on windshield clarity

* **Procedural implication:**
  Possible **category error in problem definition**

---

### Claim: “Cost reduction”

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Removal of components reduces cost meaningfully

* **Failure scenario:**

  * Systems replaced by **alternative tech (cameras, sensors)**
  * Costs shift rather than decline

* **Procedural implication:**
  Economic claim unsupported → weak RFA certification basis

---

### Claim: “Manufacturers ensure safety”

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Defect framework substitutes for prescriptive standards

* **Failure scenario:**

  * Post hoc enforcement weaker than design standards
  * Detection lag creates real-world risk

* **Procedural implication:**
  Shift from **ex ante regulation → ex post liability**
