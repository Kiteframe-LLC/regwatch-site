## Step 1 — Claim Stress Tests

### Claim: Air quality improvement is due to permanent/enforceable measures

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Emissions reductions (woodstoves, curtailment, etc.) are primary drivers

* **Failure scenario:**
  Meteorology or external factors (e.g., wildfire variability) account for a significant portion of improvement

* **Procedural implication:**
  Redesignation could be based on **correlation rather than causation**

---

### Claim: Maintenance demonstrated via inventory comparison

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Emissions inventory stability → air quality stability

* **Failure scenario:**
  Small increases in NH₃/SO₂ or nonlinear atmospheric chemistry produce exceedances

* **Procedural implication:**
  Maintenance plan may be **under-sensitive to precursor dynamics**

---

### Claim: Advisory system changes are equivalent

* **Underlying assumption:**
  Behavioral response to advisory language ≈ response to prior color system

* **Failure scenario:**
  Public compliance differs → emissions reduction differs

* **Procedural implication:**
  Enforcement effectiveness may degrade without being detected
