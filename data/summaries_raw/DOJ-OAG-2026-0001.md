# Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

---

## Claim

AG first-instance review of bar complaints.

### Underlying assumption

Federal review is necessary to prevent improper state interference.

### Negation test

If state bars can fairly investigate federal lawyers without interfering with federal duties:

* the federal review layer becomes unnecessary
* rule primarily delays bar oversight.

### Counterfactual test

Variation scenarios:

* complaint involving misconduct unrelated to federal duties
* attorney no longer employed by DOJ
* misconduct affecting state court proceedings

Under these conditions, federal pre-review may weaken state disciplinary authority.

### Adversarial interpretation

DOJ attorneys accused of misconduct could:

* delay investigations
* restrict access to information
* leverage federal review to stall bar discipline.

### Procedural implication

Possible **disciplinary delay mechanism**.

---

## Claim

Most bars already defer to OPR.

### Underlying assumption

The rule merely formalizes existing behavior.

### Failure scenario

If many bars do **not** defer in practice:

* rule materially alters the balance of authority.

### Procedural implication

Record lacks empirical evidence.

---

## Claim

Rule deters politically motivated complaints.

### Underlying assumption

Significant abuse of bar complaints exists.

### Failure scenario

If abuse is rare:

* rule may suppress legitimate ethics oversight.

### Procedural implication

Rule justification may be unsupported.

# Step 2 — Essence of the Rule

### Regulatory action

Create **DOJ first-review authority over state bar complaints** against DOJ attorneys.

### Key mechanism

Before state bar investigation begins:

```
Attorney General / OPR reviews allegations
↓
DOJ may request bar to suspend investigation
↓
DOJ completes internal review
↓
Bar may resume investigation
```

Evidence:

> “the Attorney General shall have the right to review the allegations in the first instance.” 

---

### Affected entities

Observed Fact

* DOJ attorneys
* State bar disciplinary authorities
* Office of Professional Responsibility (OPR)

---

### Statutory authority

Primary statute:

```
28 U.S.C. 530B
```

Which requires DOJ attorneys to follow state professional rules. 

---

### Key regulatory change

Current system:

```
State bar investigates misconduct directly.
```

Proposed system:

```
DOJ internal review precedes bar investigation.
```
