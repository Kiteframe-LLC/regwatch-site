# Step 1 — Claim Stress Test

---

### Claim

Emphasizing performance over tenure improves merit-based retention.

**Underlying assumption**

Performance ratings reliably reflect employee merit.

**Failure scenario**

If ratings are:

* inflated
* inconsistent across agencies
* politically influenced

then performance weighting may distort retention outcomes.

**Procedural implication**

The rule may rely on **administratively variable metrics** without demonstrating reliability.

---

### Claim

Performance-credit scoring improves RIF outcomes.

**Underlying assumption**

Quantitative scoring meaningfully distinguishes employee value.

**Failure scenario**

Ratings distributions cluster tightly (e.g., most employees rated “fully successful”).

**Procedural implication**

The rule’s scoring approach may produce **minimal differentiation**, undermining the stated goal.

---

### Claim

Agency discretion improves flexibility.

**Underlying assumption**

Agency leadership will apply discretion neutrally.

**Failure scenario**

Managers apply discretionary scoring in ways that favor preferred personnel.

**Procedural implication**

Potential for **perceived politicization of RIF decisions**.

---

# Step 2 — Essence of the Rule

### Observed Facts

The rule:

* revises federal **reduction-in-force regulations**
* modifies **retention ranking methodology**
* increases weight given to **performance ratings**
* reduces relative importance of **tenure and length of service**
* allows agencies to assign **additional performance credit**
* modifies related programs:

  * Reemployment Priority List (RPL)
  * CTAP / ICTAP
  * transfer of function
  * furlough procedures

Evidence:

> “prioritizing performance over tenure and length of service when determining which employees will be retained in a RIF.” 

Statutory authorities cited include:

```
5 U.S.C. 1103
5 U.S.C. 1302
5 U.S.C. 3502
```

---

### Inference

The rule seeks to **shift the federal RIF retention hierarchy** toward performance-based evaluation rather than seniority-based protection.
